about

I'm a a front-end web developer and new media journalist in Toronto and currently a full-time front-end development student at HackerYou. I also hold a bachelor of journalism degree from Ryerson University, where I majored in print journalism.

My previous work experience includes six years at CBCNews.ca in a variety of roles, including web content production and web development. Specific projects there included producing interactives and photo galleries and covering social media issues during the 2008 and 2011 federal election campaigns. I was also the digital project lead on CBCNews.ca's coverage of Parks Canada's 2012 search for the Franklin expedition wrecks, and held a high-impact producer contract between 2012 and 2013.

I live in Toronto with my husband and our extensive book collection. In my spare time I enjoy needlework, attempting to resume running after a two-year hiatus, and working my way through the RCM piano curriculum.
